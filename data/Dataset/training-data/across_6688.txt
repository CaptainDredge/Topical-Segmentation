NEW YORK  (Reuters) - Standard & Poor's on Tuesday said it will likely downgrade a large number of credit derivative deals with exposure to American International Group ( AIG.N ) and Lehman Brothers LEH.N after Lehman defaulted and AIG was downgraded.Both companies were included in a large number of so-called synthetic collateralized debt obligations (CDOs), which in turn will pressure the ratings of the deals, S&P said in a statement.

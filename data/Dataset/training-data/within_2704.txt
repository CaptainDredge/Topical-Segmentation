Analysts, on average, expected earnings of 77 cents a share, according to Reuters Estimates. Customer spending growth was generally strong, and credit quality showed few signs of weakening. The average card member spent $2,985 during the quarter, up from $2,778 a year earlier. The number of outstanding American Express and related cards rose by 1.5 million during the quarter to 78 million.

-----

In the year ended September 30 2006, U.S. personal bankruptcy filings were 1.085 million, down 37.9 percent from the previous year. A law that took effect on October 17 made it tougher for many individuals to have their debts excused in bankruptcy. American Express' percentage of accounts 90 days past due as a percentage of total is 1.8 percent, up from 1.6 percent in the fourth quarter of 2005, but still low by historical standards.

-----

American Express is growing internationally, as well. In November, the company launched the first Amex branded corporate card in China with Industrial & Commercial Bank of China ( 1398.HK ). Amex owns a 0.4 percent stake in ICBC. "We're very bullish on China," Gary Crittenden, American Express's chief financial officer, said on a conference call.

-----

The New York-based company has done a good job of giving rewards points, or incentives for customers to use their American Express cards, without paying too much for them, said David Roberts, publisher of the Nilson Report, a credit card industry newsletter. "It's hard work, but they're staying ahead of the rest of the industry," Roberts said.

NEW YORK  (Reuters) - Apparel retailer Abercrombie & Fitch Co ( ANF.N ) said on Thursday its sales at stores open at least a year rose 6 percent in April.Analysts, on average, had expected an increase of 2 percent, according to Reuters Estimates. The New Albany, Ohio-based company, which owns the Abercrombie & Fitch, Hollister and RUEHL chains, said net sales rose to $241.0 million for the four-week period ended May 3, from $204.6 million in the comparable period a year earlier.

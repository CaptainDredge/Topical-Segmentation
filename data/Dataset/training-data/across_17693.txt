Gross revenue for the latest quarter rose 13 percent to $1.70 billion. Excluding traffic acquisition costs (TAC) -- the cut of revenue Yahoo pays to marketing partners who run ads on its systems -- net revenue grew 15 percent to $1.23 billion. Excluding one-time items, Yahoo reported earnings per share of 16 cents.

An Ecuadorean court found that Texaco, bought by Chevron in 2001, contaminated the area from 1964 to 1992. Damages were increased to $19 billion in July. Chevron says Texaco cleaned up all waste pits for which it was responsible before turning the sites over to state-owned oil company Petroecuador, which still operates in the area.

(Reuters) - Below are the highlights of RHJ International's ( RHJI.BR ) "Project Beam" concept for General Motors unit Opel, a copy of which was obtained by Reuters on Friday:CONSIDERATION AND OWNERSHIP - RHJ contributes 175 million euros at closing of the deal, plus 100 million on Dec 31, 2012, for 50.1 percent equity stake - GM has 39.9 percent stake, no cash contribution - Employees have 10 percent stake, contribution of 250-300 million euros savings per year ASSUMPTIONS - Royalty rate of 3.5 percent in years 2010-2012, thereafter 5 percent - 3.8 billion euros in loans guaranteed by German government, to be repaid by 2014 - No exit by RHJ or dividends until government-backed loans have been repaid in full HEADCOUNT - Overall headcount reduced by about 9,900 employees throughout Europe, of which 8.1 million in manufacturing - Antwerp closed in March 2010 - Bochum kept open but downsized to produce the new Zafira, reduction to two shifts up to 2011 - Eisenach idled and reopened in 2012 to produce Corsa HB3 - Luton downsized in case Renault takes out its volume, 25 million euros separation costs included in plan - Ruesselsheim downsized as volume is moved to Bochum - Zaragoza downsized in order to re-open Eisenach in 2012 FINANCIALS - Net sales/revenues projected to rise from 16.9 billion euros in 2009 to 17.8 billion in 2010, 20.6 billion in 2011, 23.3 billion in 2012, 24.1 billion in 2013, 23.6 billion in 2014 - EBITDA projected at -0.7 billion euros in 2009, 0.5 billion in 2010, 1.7 billion in 2011, 2.4 billion in 2012, 2.3 billion in 2013, 2.5 billion in 2014 (Reporting by  Gernot Heller ; Writing by  Noah Barkin )

(Reuters) - Swiss bank Credit Suisse ( CSGN.VX ) and reinsurer Swiss Re RUKN.VX both announced the appointment of new chairmen on Monday, shifting executives in an effort to limit the impact of the financial crisis.Here are some facts about the designated new chairmen at the two Swiss financial companies: HANS-ULRICH DOERIG, NOMINATED CREDIT SUISSE CHAIRMAN * Swiss citizen, born in 1940 * Studied at St. Gallen University, has degrees in economics (doctorate) and law * Lecturer at the University of Zurich and the Swiss Banking School * Started his career at J.P. Morgan in New York * Joined Credit Suisse in 1973 and held various management positions, becoming vice-chairman and chairman of the risk committee in 2003 WALTER KIELHOLZ, NOMINATED SWISS RE CHAIRMAN * Born Feb 25, 1951 in Zurich * Studied business administration at St. Gallen University * Is married and a Swiss citizen * Has over 30 years experience in reinsurance industry, joining Swiss Re in 1989 and serving as chief executive from 1997 to 2002 * Was Credit Suisse chairman until stepping down to focus on new role as chairman at Swiss Re (Reporting by  Sam Cage  and  Katie Reid ; editing by Hans Peters)

FRANKFURT  (Reuters) - BMW ( BMWG.DE ), the world's largest premium carmaker, has sold about 1.8 million vehicles in 2012, its chief financial officer told a German newspaper."One of our goals was to increase vehicle sales in 2012 and to reach a new record in deliveries. With about 1.8 million vehicles, we have achieved this," the executive, Friedrich Eichiner, told Die Welt in an interview.

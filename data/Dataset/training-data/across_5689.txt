WILMINGTON, Delaware  (Reuters) - A hedge fund is taking aim at the world's biggest banks in an effort to recoup $1.2 billion it lost on subprime mortgages, entering a legal fight where so far Wall Street has largely been unscathed.The lawsuit by Cambridge Place Investment Management against Morgan Stanley ( MS.N ), Goldman Sachs Group Inc ( GS.N ) and about 10 other banks, is one of the biggest cases of its kind to be filed so far in U.S. courts.

( STZ.N ) posted a quarterly profit that topped estimates on Thursday, as a lower-than-expected tax rate offset a decline in operating margin due to higher material costs and pricing pressure in Britain's wine market.The owner of Robert Mondavi, Vendage and Ravenswood wines, whose shares rose 3 percent in morning trading, said its comparable tax rate for the fourth quarter was 32.9 percent versus 24.8 percent a year ago.

London Brent crude rose $2.48 a barrel to $78.34. Third-quarter U.S. gross domestic product grew at a rate of 3.5 percent, beating analysts' expectations for 3.3 percent growth. In another sign that demand in the world's largest fuel consumer could rise, the number of U.S. workers filing new claims for jobless benefits dipped by 1,000 last week.

-----

NEW YORK  (Reuters) - Oil rose nearly $3 to top $80 a barrel on Thursday after news that the U.S. economy returned to growth in the third quarter, reviving expectations of an increase in fuel consumption.U.S. crude oil futures rose $2.70 to $80.16 a barrel by 2:01 p.m. EDT (1801 GMT).

-----

"Oil futures are up sharply on the GDP data, on optimism that economic conditions will improve and raise demand for petroleum products," said Andy Lebow, broker at MF Global in New York. Wall Street also rose, as corporate earnings topped expectations. .N Higher equities and a weaker dollar have pushed crude prices higher in recent months as investors look to wider macro economic data for signs of economic recovery and a potential rise in energy demand.

-----

Crude prices have more than doubled since the turn of the year. However, prices remain far off the peak near $150 a barrel hit in July 2008, when booming demand from powerhouses like China sparked fears supplies may struggle to keep up.

(Reuters) - Federal Reserve officials discussed the possibility of launching a fresh round of bond buying before deciding last month on a smaller step to aid the economy.A majority of Fed policymakers backed the $400 billion stimulus program, known as "Operation Twist," under which the Fed sells short-term securities and buys longer-term bonds to drive down long-term interest rates.

-----

Three Fed officials dissented, saying more easing is not warranted. Their objections underscore the hurdles Fed Chairman Ben Bernanke faces in winning support for any additional steps to stimulate the economy.

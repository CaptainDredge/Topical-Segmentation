Earlier on Tuesday, the Conference Board said its U.S. consumer confidence index rose to 51.9 in July from 51.0 in June. Economists were expecting a reading of 50.0. The Consumer Comfort Index components were all lower from the previous week. Positive views on personal finances fell 3 percentage points to 49 percent and those on the buying climate lost 2 percentage points to 21 percent.

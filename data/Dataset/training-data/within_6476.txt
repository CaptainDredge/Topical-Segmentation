CHICAGO  (Reuters) - Chocolate maker Hershey Co. ( HSY.N ) on Thursday lowered its 2007 earnings forecast as higher dairy costs put additional pressure on a company that is spending more money on marketing and on new products such as dark chocolate bars.The stock fell more than 4 percent on the warning.

Hershey, which has lost U.S. market share to Mars Inc., has also been overhauling its supply chain and manufacturing system to try to improve margins. "While we've experienced some areas of improvement in the U.S. business, we're not as far along as we need to be," Richard Lenny, chief executive officer, said in a news release.

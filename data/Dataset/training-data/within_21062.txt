DETROIT  (Reuters) - May auto sales fell 14 percent from a year earlier on an adjusted basis, according to data from auto manufacturers on Tuesday. The following are highlights from the largest carmakers in the U.S. market.Overall industry * Total light vehicle sales -- 1.397 million units. General Motors Corp * Light vehicle sales down adjusted 30 percent (272,363 units), unadjusted sales down 28 percent * Year-to-date sales down 17 percent * Inventories at month-end: 772,000 units, down about 219,000 vehicles from a year earlier * Cars/trucks: Car sales down adjusted 17 percent/light truck sales down 39 percent * Market share: 19.1 percent * Key vehicles: Chevrolet Impala down 35.7 percent, Chevrolet Malibu up 33.9 percent, Chevrolet Silverado down 44.1 percent, Chevrolet Tahoe down 42 percent, GMC Sierra down 33.9 percent.

-----

* Key vehicles: Jeep Wrangler down 25 percent, Dodge Ram pickup down 37 percent, Dodge Caliber up 7 percent, all on an unadjusted basis. * Incentives: average $3,714 per vehicle, down from $3,823 a year earlier. Honda Motor Co * Vehicle sales up an adjusted 11.3 percent (167,997 units), unadjusted sales up 16 percent * Year-to-date sales up an adjusted 3.1 percent * Cars/trucks: cars up 27 percent, trucks down 12 percent * Market share: 12 percent * Key vehicles: Civic up 28.3 percent, Fit up 47.4 percent, Accord up 31.9 percent, Pilot down 19.7 percent, Odyssey down 18.0 percent, CR-V down 1.5 percent * Incentives: average $1,256 per vehicle, down from $1,383 a year earlier Nissan Motor Co Ltd * Car and light truck sales up an adjusted 4.4 percent year over year (100,874 units); up 8 percent unadjusted.

-----

* Incentives: averaged $3,326 per vehicle, up from $2,964 a year earlier. Chrysler LLC * Car and light truck sales down adjusted 28 percent (148,747 units), unadjusted sales down 25 percent * Cars/trucks: Car sales down 31 percent/truck sales down 27 percent on an adjusted basis. * Market share: 10.6 percent * Inventories at month-end were 412,009 units, or a 75-day supply, down 14 percent from year ago.

Sources: Autodata Corp and industry tracking firm Edmunds.com for incentives and broad sales and market share comparisons in adjusted terms to account for one more selling day in May compared with a year earlier, and company releases.

- Panasonic ( 6752.T ) said on Tuesday continuing aftershocks were preventing it from inspecting factories in northern Japan, including those making optical pick-ups and other electronic parts, digital cameras and audio equipment. None of the manufacturing facilities was badly damaged but it would take time to resume operations as infrastructure needed to be restored, a company spokesman said.

-----

(Reuters) - The following is a roundup of the effect on auto makers and electronics makers following Friday's devastating earthquake and tsunami.AUTO MAKERS - Toyota Motor Co ( 7203.T ) said production still stopped at all its 12 Japan factories until at least Wednesday. A three-day production halt would reduce output by 40,000 vehicles.

-----

The company also stopped scheduling overtime work in its North America plants. - Honda Motor Co ( 7267.T ) reiterated its plans to suspend all production in Japan until at least Sunday. Honda manufactured 69,170 cars in January in Japan, where it made 24 percent of its cars. - Nissan Motor Co ( 7201.T ) said output is stopped at all four of its car assembly factories in Japan.

-----

- Mazda Motor Corp ( 7261.T ) said it still plans to extend the suspension of production at two plants in southwestern Japan to March 20, but has not decided on how to proceed after that. - Fuji Heavy Industries Co ( 7270.T ) said all five of its car and car parts-related plants for its Subaru-brand vehicles in Gunma prefecture, north of Tokyo, will be closed until at least Sunday.

-----

ELECTRONICS MAKERS: - Sony Corp ( 6758.T ) opened one factory, which makes optical films, used in LCDs and also manufactures adhesives, on Wednesday. Seven plants, which make an array of devices from IC cards to blu ray discs to lithium batteries, remain closed. Sony is not sure when the plans will resume operations.

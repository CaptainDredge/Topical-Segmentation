NEW YORK  (Reuters) - Apparel maker VF Corp. <VFC.N on Tuesday posted an 8 percent rise in quarterly net income, above expectations, helped by stronger sales in its outdoor wear, jeans, uniforms and corporate-wear businesses.Shares of the company rose 3 percent in midafternoon trade. The maker of Wrangler jeans, Nautica sportswear and North Face coats said first-quarter net income was $138.3 million, or $1.20 per share, compared with $128.2 million, or $1.14 per share a year earlier.

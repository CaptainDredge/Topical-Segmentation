WASHINGTON  (Reuters) - President Barack Obama spoke about the  BP oil spill in a speech from the Oval Office on Tuesday evening, laying out a "battle plan" for going forward.Here are some key points Obama raised in the televised address to the nation. * Said oil spill is worst environmental disaster U.S. has ever faced * Vowed to fight the spill "with everything we've got for as long it takes" * Will make BP pay for the damage and will do whatever's necessary to help the Gulf Coast and its people recover * Will tell BP chairman company must establish fund to compensate workers and business owners affected by spill * Compensation fund must be administered by an independent, third party * Has established a commission to determine cause of spill and recommend new safety and environmental standards * Defended moratorium on deepwater drilling, saying we need to know the facts before we allow deepwater drilling to continue * Has directed BP to mobilize additional equipment and technology that should capture up to 90 percent of the oil leaking out of the well * Likened spill to an epidemic that "we will be fighting for months and even years" * Installing new leadership at oil industry watchdog the Minerals Management Service * Oil spill a "painful and powerful" reminder that the time to embrace a clean energy future is now * U.S. can't afford not to change how it produces and uses energy * Said now is the moment for this generation to embark on a national mission to unleash American innovation and seize control of our own destiny * Urged governors in the affected states to activate National Guard members authorized to help in response effort (Reporting by JoAnne Allen; Editing by Eric Walsh

CHICAGO  (Reuters) - TRW Automotive Holdings Corp. ( TRW.N ) reported a 44 percent drop in quarterly earnings on Thursday under pressure from automaker production cuts in North America, but exceeded average Wall Street forecasts and shares rose as much as 2.2 percent to an all-time high.TRW also warned that output cuts by automakers in North America, price reductions on its parts, commodity cost pressures and expectations for significantly lower heavy truck sales would pose challenges in 2007.

-----

TRW -- which makes safety products such as airbags, electronic stability controls and tire pressure monitoring equipment -- has been closing plants and shifting production to lower-cost regions in response to industry pressures. "Given the production environment, the company posted solid results for the quarter and full-year," Morningstar analyst John Novak said.

-----

TRW expects Big 3 production to be down about 4 percent in 2007, with a 9.5 percent drop in the first half. TRW forecast 2007 earnings per share of $1.85 to $2.15, while analysts on average expect $2.11. It forecast sales of $13.4 billion to $13.8 billion, while analysts expect $13.38 billion.

-----

Net earnings fell to $33 million, or 32 cents per share, in the fourth quarter from $59 million, or 57 cents per share, a year earlier. Revenue rose 4.3 percent to $3.27 billion Excluding a tax benefit, TRW earned 16 cents per share. Analysts, on average, had expected 3 cents per share on revenue of $3.15 billion, according to Reuters Estimates.

-----

It anticipates first-quarter sales of $3.5 billion, while analysts are looking for $3.43 billion. TRW shares were up 35 cents, or 1.17 percent, at $30.28 on the New York Stock Exchange. The stock's close at $29.93 Wednesday was its highest since TRW started trading publicly in February 2004. Through Wednesday, the shares had risen 15.7 percent this year, compared with a 12 percent rise in the Dow Jones U.S. automobiles and parts index .DJUSAP.

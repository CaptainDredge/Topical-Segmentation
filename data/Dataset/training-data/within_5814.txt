The company, founded by defense industry veteran Frank Lanza in 1997, has built itself into the Pentagon's No. 7 contractor after a series of acquisitions.

-----

L-3 said both acquisitions would add to earnings and that it now expects full-year earnings of $5.65 to $5.75 per share, up 10 cents from its previous forecast. Wall Street is expecting $5.69 per share, on average. L-3 forecast 2007 sales of $13.2 billion to $13.3 billion, up from a range of $12.9 billion to $13.1 billion.

-----

The New York-based company posted a quarterly net profit of $162 million, or $1.29 per share, compared with $139 million, or $1.13 per share, a year earlier. That was just ahead of Wall Street's average forecast of $1.28 per share, according to Reuters Estimates. Sales rose 14 percent to $3.3 billion.

-----

Lanza died last year and was succeeded by Michael Strianese. It said on Monday it had extended its acquisitions, buying Geneva Aerospace Inc. -- which makes technology for unmanned aerial vehicles -- in January, for an undisclosed price. It said it had also agreed to buy Global Communications Solutions Inc., which makes portable communications devices.

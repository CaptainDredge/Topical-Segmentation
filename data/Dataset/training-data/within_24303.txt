LOWER FORECAST For the full year, Staples forecast low-single-digit sales growth and earnings per share flat with last year's adjusted earnings of $1.42 per share, excluding the Corporate Express acquisition and other items. In May, the company had stood by its prior outlook, which called for mid-single-digit sales growth and high-single-digit earnings per share growth for the full year.

Staples also forecast savings from its $2.65 billion purchase of Dutch peer Corporate Express that were lighter than some analysts had expected. Sales of office supplies have suffered as small-business customers cut back spending due to a weakened U.S. economy, the credit crisis, higher commodity costs and the decline in housing values.

NEW YORK  (Reuters) - Staples Inc ( SPLS.O ) said on Tuesday it would report quarterly results below Wall Street expectations and cut its full-year outlook, sending shares down as much as 10 percent.The world's largest office products retailer cited weak sales in North America and Europe, stoking investor pessimism about smaller rivals Office Depot Inc ( ODP.N ) and OfficeMax Inc OMX.N, shares of which also fell.

-----

Analysts on average have expected 25 cents per share, according to Reuters Estimates. Staples shares were down $1.04, or 4.2 percent at $23.54 in late trading on the Nasdaq after falling as low as $22.05. On the New York Stock Exchange, shares of Office Depot were down 5.7 percent at $6.41 while OfficeMax shares were down 4.5 percent at $13.59.

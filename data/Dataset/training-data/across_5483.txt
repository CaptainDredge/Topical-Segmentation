NEW YORK  (Reuters) - Capstead Mortgage Corp ( CMO.N ) said on Tuesday that it expected to have sufficient liquidity to manage its $7.3 billion portfolio absent any significant drop in financing availability, but it would take a $1.5 million first-quarter loss from a recent sale of $760 million of debt.The announcement suggests easing pressure on the agency real estate investment trust, which invests in mortgages backed by Fannie Mae FNM.N and Freddie Mac FRE.N. In a U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission filing, Dallas-based Capstead said recent U.S. Federal Reserve actions to support the mortgage securities market had in the last week "largely" alleviated market concerns about the pricing of agency-guaranteed mortgage debt.

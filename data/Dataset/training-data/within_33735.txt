NEW YORK  (Reuters) - Honeywell International ( HON.N ) stood by its 2009 profit forecast despite a "much tougher" macroeconomic environment, the diversified U.S. manufacturer said on Monday.Chief Executive Dave Cote affirmed the company's 2009 earnings forecast of $3.20 to $3.55 per share during a presentation to investors that was monitored over the Internet.

-----

Honeywell's shares have fallen about 50 percent over the past year, a slightly more modest decline than the 56 percent posted by the Standard & Poor's capital goods industry group .GSPIC. Honeywell's competitors include United Technologies Corp ( UTX.N ) in aerospace and building control systems, Goodrich Corp GR.N in aviation, and DuPont Co ( DFT.N ) in specialty materials.

-----

Analysts had predicted 2009 earnings of $3.10 per share. The Morris Township, New Jersey-based company lowered its 2009 revenue outlook to a range of $33.3 billion to $35.1 billion. It previously had forecast $33.6 billion to $35.3 billion. According to a forecast by the Manufacturers Alliance/MAPI, the global recession in manufacturing looks to be the deepest and longest since 1973-1974, with production falling 9.2 percent in 2009 after a 2.5 percent decline in 2008.
